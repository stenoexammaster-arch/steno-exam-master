Stenography & typing tips

Practical tips for exam-oriented practice

Small corrections in technique can create a big difference in your final speed and accuracy.

‚å®Ô∏è Typing Tips

Focus on building a clean and consistent typing style before chasing very high speeds.

  • Focus on accuracy first ‚Äì maintain at least 95‚Äì98% accuracy before pushing for higher WPM. In exams, high speed with low accuracy is not useful.
  • Maintain proper spacing ‚Äì avoid extra spaces before commas, full stops and brackets. Use one space between words and after punctuation.
  • Avoid unnecessary backspace ‚Äì frequent corrections break rhythm. Correct only when absolutely required in exam-style practice.
  • Develop typing rhythm ‚Äì press keys softly and steadily instead of hitting them hard. A smooth rhythm reduces fatigue and increases consistency.
  • Use home-row position ‚Äì always return fingers to ASDF / JKL; for English or the central row for Hindi layouts.

üìù Steno Tips

Stenography requires smart practice with clear rules, not just raw speed.

  • Follow exam-specific rules ‚Äì each board (SSC, HSSC, Court, etc.) has its own marking scheme. Practise with the same rules enabled in Steno Exam Master.
  • Revise mistakes after every test ‚Äì immediately repeat dictation for lines where strokes were missed or wrongly transcribed.
  • Practice with real exam format ‚Äì same speed, same passage length, and same transcription time as your actual exam.
  • Time management is key ‚Äì divide your time between shorthand practice, transcription and error review. Do not spend your full time only on new outlines.
  • Maintain neat notebooks ‚Äì clear strokes and spacing help you read faster during transcription, reducing typing mistakes.
Rule awareness

English & Hindi typing rules used in checking

Understanding checking logic is as important as speed. Steno Exam Master follows exam-oriented rules for both English and Hindi papers.

In government exams, wrong use of capital letters is generally treated as a full mistake, especially in:

  • Starting letter of sentences.
  • Names of persons, departments, designations and places.
  • Abbreviations (e.g. SSC, HSSC, CPCT, Govt., Ltd.).

Always carefully match the original passage. Do not randomly capitalise or convert capitals to small letters. During practice, slow down slightly whenever you see a capital letter coming next.

Extra or missing spaces are a very common reason for losing marks. Exams generally follow these principles:

  • Single space between each word ‚Äì never press space twice.
  • No space before comma, full stop, question mark or colon.
  • One space after punctuation marks (except before closing brackets).
  • In Hindi, avoid breaking words in the middle by extra spacing around matras.

While practising, keep your thumb movement consistent and avoid tapping spacebar unnecessarily when you feel nervous.

Missing or wrong punctuation can change the meaning of a sentence and is almost always counted as a full mistake.

  • Use the correct symbol ‚Äì comma (,), full stop (.), colon (:), semicolon (;) and hyphen (-) as shown in the passage.
  • Keep brackets, quotation marks and slashes in the same order as original.
  • In Hindi, do not replace the ‚Äú‡•§‚Äù (danda) with an English dot.

During revision, quickly scan only punctuation marks while ignoring words. This helps you catch punctuation errors separately from spelling mistakes.

Hindi typing mistakes mostly occur in anusvaar (‡§Ç), matras and conjunct (half) letters.

  • Anusvaar (‡§Ç) ‚Äì used for nasal sound. Using an extra or missing anusvaar is usually treated as a full mistake.
  • Half letters (‡•ç) ‚Äì created using ‚Äúhalant/virama‚Äù. Wrong use in conjuncts like ‡§ï‡•ç‡§§, ‡§§‡•ç‡§∞, ‡§ú‡•ç‡§û, ‡§∂‡•ç‡§∞ results in spelling errors.
  • Matras ‚Äì ‡§ó‡§≤‡§§‡•Ä in matras (‡§æ,‡§ø,‡•Ä,‡•Å,‡•Ç,‡•á,‡•à,‡•ã,‡•å) is counted as spelling mistake, even if the base consonant is correct.

Always practise with the same keyboard layout that is used in your real exam (Inscript, Remington, Kruti Dev, etc.) to avoid confusion in matras and conjuncts.

Steno Exam Master aims to follow the checking style of government exam boards:

  • Full mistakes ‚Äì wrong word, missing word, extra word, wrong capitalisation, wrong punctuation, major spelling difference.
  • Half mistakes ‚Äì minor spelling variations, acceptable alternatives where boards give partial penalty.
  • Ignored mistakes ‚Äì differences which are normally ignored by examiners (e.g. some spacing issues within limits, allowed abbreviations).

This logic is updated according to the selected exam so that your practice result is as close as possible to the real evaluation.
